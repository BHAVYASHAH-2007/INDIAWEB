Key Aspects of Pateti:

  • Repentance and Reflection:

    • Pateti is derived from the word "Patet," meaning repentance. Zoroastrians use this day to reflect on their actions and seek forgiveness for any wrongdoings, bad thoughts, words, or deeds.

  • Religious Observances:

    • Parsis visit fire temples (Agiaries) to offer prayers and seek blessings. The sacred fire, representing God, is central to Zoroastrian worship.

  • Celebrations:

    While Pateti is a day of introspection, it's also a time for celebration. Parsis wear new clothes, decorate their homes with flowers and rangolis, and prepare traditional Parsi dishes.

  • Traditional Foods:

    A variety of Parsi dishes are prepared, including Ravo, Sev, Sali Boti, Patra ni Macchi, and Pulao Dal.

  • Community and Family:

    Pateti is a time for Parsis to come together, strengthen bonds, and celebrate with family and friends.

  • Four F's:

    The festivities often revolve around the "Four F's": Fire, Fragrance, Food, and Friendship.
